## Overview
MotsDits uses a warm Quebec sketchbook identity: pastel blues, cream paper, hand-drawn outlines, light hatching, and conversational tutoiement. The site should feel local, private, and human rather than technical.

## Colors
- Background: `#E8EDF5`
- Paper surface: `#FAFAF7`
- Warm paper section: `#FCF4DC`
- Cream cards: `#F8EBC8`
- Text and sketch borders: `#5A6478`
- Titles and outlines: `#3E6FA0`
- Primary action: `#8FB4D9`
- Soft fill: `#A5C8E1`
- Quebec accent: `#0C45A1`, used only once as a discreet accent
- Success: `#8FB29A`
- Warning: `#E8B488`
- Danger: `#E89B9B`
- Highlight tape: `#F0D88B`

## Typography
- Display: Caveat, large handwritten headings, weight 700, tight line-height.
- Body: Quattrocento, 18px base, relaxed reading rhythm.
- UI: Nunito, bold labels, navigation, buttons, and captions.
- Mono: Cascadia Mono when needed, at roughly 90% opacity.

## Rounded
Use asymmetric radii rather than perfect corners. Typical cards use `14px 22px 16px 19px`; sticky nav uses `18px 14px 20px 16px`; CTAs use pill radii.

## Spacing
Mobile sections start around 54px vertical padding; desktop sections use around 78px. Card grids use 22px gaps. Hero and two-column sections use 34–42px gaps.

## Components
- Sketch card: paper or cream surface, 1.8px charcoal border, light diagonal hatching, soft shadow.
- Primary button: blue pill, charcoal outline, soft shadow, slight hover rotation.
- Section number: small hand-drawn circle with Caveat numbering.
- FAQ row: native details/summary accordion with sketched chevron.
- Image placeholder: labelled A–G with commissioning caption and tape strip.

## Do's and Don'ts
Do write in Quebec French with tutoiement. Do keep image placeholders descriptive for commissioning. Don’t use gradients, neon glow, stock photos, generic SaaS jargon, or English copy in the French version except required product names, vendor names, and the explicit placeholder labels.
